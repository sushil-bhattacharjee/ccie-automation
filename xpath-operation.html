🔧 How to Build Predicates

Predicates go inside [] and filter nodes without changing which node you're on. Three patterns:

1. Chained predicates [][] — implicit AND

'/interfaces/interface[starts-with(name,"Gigabit")][state/oper-status="UP"]'

Each [] is a filter applied in sequence. ALL must be true. Equivalent to and.

2. and / or inside a single predicate

# AND — same result as chaining
'[starts-with(name,"Gigabit") and state/oper-status="UP"]'

# OR — only possible inside [], cannot chain with [][]
'[as="65002" or as="65003"]'

3. Parentheses () for grouping — controls precedence

# Without (): "and" binds tighter — could give wrong results
# With (): OR is evaluated first, then AND
'[(as="65002" or as="65003") and connection/state="established"]'

Quick Reference

[][]AND only (chain filters)[pred1][pred2]
[A and B]AND inside one predicate[status="UP" and pkts>0]
[A or B]OR (only works inside [])[as="65002" or as="65003"]
[(A or B) and C]Grouped logic[(as="65002" or as="65003") and state="established"]

Invalid Syntax — or between predicates

# WRONG — "or" between ][ is not XPath syntax
'[as="65002"] or [as="65003"]'

# WRONG — "and" between ][ with literal keyword
'[as="65002"] and [state="established"]'

# RIGHT — everything inside one [] or use [][] for AND
'[as="65002" or as="65003"]'
'[as="65002"][state="established"]'
⚠️ Think of [] as a filter box. All logic (and, or, ()) goes inside the box. Multiple boxes [][] are always AND.
